Blood Donation Eligibility Criteria

To donate blood safely and responsibly, donors must meet the following requirements:

✅ Basic Eligibility

To donate safely, you must meet these basic criteria:

Age

Generally 18 to 65 years old. Some areas allow 16–17 year-olds with parental consent.

Weight

Minimum weight of 45–50 kg (approximately 100–110 lbs) is required.

Health Status

Must be in good health, feeling well, and free from diseases that can be transmitted through blood.

Hemoglobin Level

Minimum hemoglobin level of 12.5 g/dL is required for safe blood donation.

Vital Signs

Normal pulse (50-100 bpm) and blood pressure (systolic 100-180 mmHg; diastolic 50-100 mmHg).

Identification

A valid photo ID is typically required for all donations.

⏸️ Temporary Deferrals

You may be eligible to donate after the waiting period:

Tattoos & Piercings

Usually requires a 6-month to 1-year wait before donating.

Travel History

Recent travel to malaria-prone areas or specific endemic countries requires a waiting period.

Recent Medication

Recent use of certain antibiotics or medications for infection may require a waiting period.

Illness

Donors with cold, flu, or infection must be completely symptom-free before donating.

Vaccinations

Some vaccines require a waiting period: 15 days for cholera vaccine, 1 year for rabies vaccine.

Recent Surgery & Pregnancy

Recent surgery or pregnancy typically requires a recovery period before donating is allowed.

❌ Permanent Deferrals

You cannot donate if you have the following conditions:

Infectious Diseases

HIV, Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C, or Syphilis. Any of these infections permanently disqualify donation.

Chronic Medical Conditions

Certain cancers (leukemia, lymphoma), severe heart diseases, or high-risk behaviors may permanently disqualify donors.
